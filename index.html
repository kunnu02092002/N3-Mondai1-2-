Frequently Asked Questions & JLPT N3 Study Guide

Q1: What are the most common traps in the JLPT N3 Vocabulary section?

The JLPT N3 vocabulary exam heavily relies on two specific distractors: vowel length elongation (confusing short and long sounds like Senkyo vs. Senkyō) and look-alike Kanji characters where a single radical is swapped (such as 登録 vs. 登緑). This interactive trainer is specifically designed to target these two pitfalls.

Q2: Why is typing better than multiple-choice practice for Japanese long vowels?

Multiple-choice questions allow your brain to guess by recognition. Typing out the exact Hiragana reading (like typing じょうきょう for 状況 instead of しょうぎゅう) builds physical muscle memory and forces you to actively process whether a vowel sound is elongated or short.

Q3: How do I distinguish between look-alike Kanji like 登録 and 登緑?

To master look-alike Kanji, look at the radical components. For example, 登録 (Registration) uses the metal radical (金) on the right side because it relates to historical recording plates, whereas 登緑 is a trap word using the thread radical (糸) from the color green (緑).
